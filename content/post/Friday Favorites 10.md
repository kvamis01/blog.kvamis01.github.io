Happy Friday,

[Queue the music](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F9gKVduzVEA&list=RDF9gKVduzVEA&start_radio=1), brew that pot. Minnesota’s afternoon rainstorms have been perfect for reading, writing, and sitting on the couch.

Here are 10 interesting things worth sharing this week:

1. Funny how we assume the artists we look up to must be so literary, serious, and important compared to ourselves — then learn that’s exactly how they feel about who they look up to! I’m going to have to watch [this entire Karl Ove Knausgård episode](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dr-dPzhzrxI&t=196s) from BBC’s Arts in Motion.
2. Maria and I loved watching [Desk Set](https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/desk_set) last weekend, a 1957 rom-com about a whip-smart reference librarian and the computer engineer replacing her entire research department with a computer. Very funny, and unfortunately timely with AI!
3. I’ve been reading [why people like Dante’s “Inferno”](https://blog.isakkvam.com/post/secondary-reading/) so much, so naturally I’ve been listening to Hozier’s “[Unreal Unearth](https://music.apple.com/us/album/unreal-unearth/1687585357)” album again and again.
4. “Oh Lord. I could talk about the various ways — in nature, in folklore, things like that — but honestly, the surest one? Spite.” [T. Kingfisher](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/19/books/review/t-kingfisher-wolf-worm.html) on the surest way for him to find inspiration. This is my surest way, too! [Austin Kleon](https://austinkleon.com/2019/07/16/a-negative-approach/) recommends it, too.
5. I recently read “[The Emerson Circle](https://bookshop.org/p/books/the-emerson-circle-the-concord-radicals-who-reinvented-the-world-bruce-nichols/f0e37069a0311750)” and loved learning more about the Transcendentalists, but mostly Margaret Fuller, Louisa May Alcott, and Thoreau. Just in time to watch this [new PBS miniseries](https://www.pbs.org/show/henry-david-thoreau/) on the Thoreau! [Robert Gross’ book](https://bookshop.org/p/books/the-transcendentalists-and-their-world-robert-a-gross/d78bed26eadceb6c?ean=9781250859075&next=t&next=t&affiliate=12476) is up next to read.
6. “Well, I don’t have to worry about how to pay the mortgage. I get to write the stories I want to write.” [Octavia Butler](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N2XPKRP7eSI) on the best parts about her success as a writer — the dream! I’m also an early-morning writer; by 5 p.m. my brain’s largely unhelpful and annoying.
7. I read the first Knaussgard book in 2016, and autofiction was this new, interesting genre to me. This week I [revisited a LitHub article](https://lithub.com/the-crooked-timber-of-the-mind-on-the-rise-of-autojournalism/) written by Robert Moor in 2024 (whose [new book](https://bookshop.org/p/books/in-trees-an-exploration-robert-moor/6b799894a129d75b) I’m v. excited to read) about the book “[Bjarki, not Bjarki](https://bookshop.org/p/books/bjarki-not-bjarki-on-floorboards-love-and-irreconcilable-differences-matthew-j-c-clark/e13686ec9132208a),” a great book about creativity, writing, and autojournalism.
8. Speaking of autojournalism: it’s starting to feel like summer, which means I want to float in a pool, drink gin and tonics, and read Hunter S. Thompson. [Van Neistat](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bIeYKR5BxpM&t=108s) likes him for his adventures and prose; I love him writing so [politically and entertainingly.](https://bookshop.org/p/books/freak-kingdom-hunter-s-thompson-s-manic-ten-year-crusade-against-american-fascism-timothy-denevi/d6cc9230029c9a3c)
9. [Literary fly fisherman](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/20/books/could-a-river-runs-through-it-have-been-a-hit-today.html?unlocked_article_code=1.dFA.5pkO.jFSQ2S7uOAYb&smid=url-share)! I read “A River Runs Through It” in college in two days (molecules, molecules) and loved it. “[Ninety-two in the Shade](https://bookshop.org/p/books/ninety-two-in-the-shade-thomas-mcguane/8394778a1b0e4633)” felt like Norman Maclean meets Hunter S. Thompson. I’d add [Callan Wink](https://mountainandprairie.com/callan-wink/) as a favorite modern literary fly fisherman, a form I hope continues.
10. We forget how much agency we have; [you can just email people stuff](https://substack.com/home/post/p-39048606). I’m not great at taking this advice often but it has worked well for me in the past.
