Happy Friday,

[Cue the music](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0b-_ALz35mE), brew that pot. We've had some gorgeous spring weather this month, but I think it's about to feel like full-on summer next week — so I'm squeaking in more time outside before the bugs come out.

Here are 10 interesting things worth sharing this week:

1. My favorite part of big book round-ups like The Guardian’s [100 best novels of all time](https://www.theguardian.com/books/ng-interactive/2026/may/12/the-100-best-novels-of-all-time) is [how](https://substack.com/inbox/post/198557793) many [people react](https://jaredhenderson.substack.com/p/the-guardians-100-best-english-language) with that old internet adage: [why wasn’t I consulted?](https://austinkleon.com/2019/02/03/why-wasnt-i-consulted/)
2. I agree with [David Byrne](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DYGJpmrvPk_/) that creativity comes from following what you’re passionate about. I can tell in my writing what I’m finding interesting and not!
3. Cal Newport’s book “Deep Work” had a big impact on me, and [his latest op-ed](https://archive.ph/M3VL6) about how social media has shrunk our attention spans so people are relying on AI to do their thinking for them seems on the mark.
4. New [American Masters](https://www.pbs.org/wnet/americanmasters/mary-oliver-documentary/37967/) about Mary Oliver coming out in August looks very good. [Wild Geese](https://www.poetry.com/poem/123017/wild-geese) is of course one of my favorite poems of hers, along with [Sleeping in the Forest](https://www.best-poems.net/mary_oliver/sleeping_in_the_forest.html). I have a CD of her reading her poems in my car that instantly chills me out.
5. I wasn’t familiar with Brad Neely but [his typewriter interview](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/typewriter-interview-with-brad-neely) with Austin Kleon is my favorite in the series so far.
6. Still thinking about this woman who [lived alone in a cave](https://www.npr.org/2023/04/17/1170388759/500-days-cave-beatriz-flamini-spain) for 500 days, lost count of the days after a few months, and whose response to completing the challenge was “Already? No way. I hadn't finished my book.”
7. [New Olivia Rodrigo song](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B402rKl4bUg&t=3s) reminds me of Disarm by Smashing Pumpkins and I will not patiently wait for some loud live versions to confirm.
8. TV and movie adaptations are never as good as their books — but there are still adaptations I _love_ (Little Women, Lord of the Rings, Fight Club), and the new [East of Eden miniseries](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fdBr5KyUilU) looks pretty good.
9. While I haven’t yet started a Big Summer Book, I am enjoying [Crime & Punishment](https://playbill.com/production/crime-and-punishment-a-comedy-regional-globe-theatres-old-globe-theatre-2023) (which should really just be called Crime & Overwhelming Guilt) — but Raskilnikov’s guilt and misfortune is actually just really funny to me? I must not be alone because apparently they made a [comedy stage adaptation](https://playbill.com/production/crime-and-punishment-a-comedy-regional-globe-theatres-old-globe-theatre-2023) in 2023.
10. I love how many people are into birding these days, and I loved the Listers documentary and am very, very excited the duo is working on [their next documentary](https://www.audubon.org/magazine/their-birding-documentary-became-surprise-hit-reiser-brothers-are-just-getting-started) about the ivory-billed woodpecker.
