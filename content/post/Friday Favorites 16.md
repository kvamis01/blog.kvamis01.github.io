Happy Friday,

[Cue the music](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XtDQNgevNvs), brew that pot. It’s officially summer, and I’m soaking up LONG and HUMID summer days here in Minnesota and making plans for the summer solstice.

Here are 10 interesting things worth sharing this week:

1. A [new PBS documentary](https://vimeo.com/1038311617) about the Mississippi River looks SO good, can’t wait for this to come out.
2. I love following Dave Fogler’s YouTube channel for his time-intensive, pointless house modifications — but also for the midcentury vibes and fun vlogging, and his latest on [eliminating corporate logos on his truck](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0JwvCmvaP1k) is a fun watch.
3. “I have faith that these typewriters are going to lead me somewhere. I don’t know where, but I hope somewhere interesting.” In which [Ruth Ozeki discovers the joys of a typewriter](https://lithub.com/the-sound-of-imminence-ruth-ozeki-in-praise-of-the-typewriter/). I also enjoyed learning about her [writing process journal](https://lithub.com/the-writer-you-are-is-enough-ruth-ozeki-on-process-and-acceptance/) to aid her writing process.
4. If you’re having trouble writing, try reading, watching movies, going to a museum, or going out with a friend. Austin Kleon’s book “[Don’t Call It Art](https://bookshop.org/p/books/don-t-call-it-art-10-ways-to-create-like-a-kid-again-austin-kleon/3e44c1bf468cd62e)” came out this week, and Chapter #7 is “Problems of output are problems of input.” (He’s making the book tour rounds, and I was happy to see him on the [“A Reading Life” substack](https://substack.com/home/post/p-200563811), too.)
5. I’ve been carrying a Field Notes journal in my back pocket since 2019, and I immediately ordered both sets of their new “[Explore America](https://fieldnotesbrand.com/products/explore-america)” series, which reminds me of their fantastic “[National Parks](https://fieldnotesbrand.com/products/national-parks)” series.
6. Larry McMurtry would write [five pages a day](https://famouswritingroutines.com/writing-routines/larry-mcmurtrys-writing-routine/) on his Hermes 3000 typewriter, even stopping in the middle of a sentence to avoid going over his daily limit. I loved reading the [Tracy Daugherty biography](https://bookshop.org/p/books/larry-mcmurtry-a-life-tracy-daugherty/a318690020913220) on him last summer, and the new “[Western Star](https://bookshop.org/p/books/western-star-the-life-and-legends-of-larry-mcmurtry-david-streitfeld/ec007193cc6a4acd?ean=9780063234888&next=t)” bio is on my desk now.
7. My Big Summer Book pick right now is “[Bag of Bones](https://bookshop.org/p/books/bag-of-bones-stephen-king/c6439ff6d89aa7f0?ean=9781982102494&next=t&)” (my version is 736 but it’s a small mass market paperback), and I am loving the eerie, summery tension laced throughout this one. I’m only 20% in, but I’m curious why this hasn’t been held up as one of his better books?
8. Resonated [this TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/t/ZTBU2oo4d/) about how creative backlogs can be a block. I’ve had a bunch of creative ideas rattling around in my head for too long, and doing them and moving on sounds easier and more fun than thinking “I really _should_ do that soon.” Should!
9. There’s a plethora of book clubs and read-alongs on Substack that I’m finally going to give one a try and join Many Meetings on a r[ead-through of The Silmarillion](https://substack.com/home/post/p-200005945) — a book I’ve read a few times but never feel like I quite grasp! Third time’s a charm?
10. “Doing the thing [every day](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OBtnKCG3QAE) is easier than not doing the thing every day.” I regret that rigid consistency helps me do the things I want to do but I do wish I was less all-or-nothing about it.
