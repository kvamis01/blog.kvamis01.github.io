Happy Friday,

[Cue the music](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Gl33G5wZvAU), brew that pot. I ran my first half marathon last week (it went well!) and am on a summer Stephen King kick (finished “Bag of Bones,” now starting “Joyland”).

Here are 10 interesting things worth sharing this week:

1. Love these [author completionism cards](https://substack.com/@rebeccaisabel/note/c-277213147) from Rebecca on common threads. There are a few authors I’ve read most of (Annie Dillard, Peter Heller, Robert Caro), but it’d be fun to make these cards for authors I’ve read lots of (King, Vonnegut) and authors I haven’t read yet (Baldwin, Morrison).
2. It’s tough to give a good speech that’s both interesting and meaningful, but [this two-and-a-half minute one](https://www.tiktok.com/t/ZTBKuuxL2/) from Henry Rollins of Black Flag is one of the best I’ve seen.
3. Mark Slutsky writes about the [feeling of being in good hands](https://www.markslutsky.com/p/something-good-82-in-good-hands), “an intuitive sense that the author knows what they are doing and that the experience will be worth my time.” For me, that’s cracking open Robert Caro, Gabriel Garcia Marquez, and Larry McMurtry.
4. Despite knowing a few Marvin Gaye tunes, I’d never listened to “[What’s Going On](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=URiP9dMcQhU)” which is #1 on Rolling Stones “[500 Greatest Albums of All Time.](https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-lists/best-albums-of-all-time-1062063/marvin-gaye-whats-going-on-4-1063232/)” It’s very moody, contemplative, and political! Gaye wrote it in one month in 1969 amidst police brutality and Vietnam protests saying “I work best under pressure and when I’m depressed. The world’s never been as depressing as it is right now. We’re killing the planet, killing young men in the streets, and going to war around the world. Human rights … that’s the theme.” Things change and don’t change.
5. Loving [Yumi Sakugawa’s](https://www.instagram.com/yumisakugawa?igsh=MWNucTF2cWc0ZzFoZg==) 5-point manifesto “[Discipline as Pleasure in Creative Practice](https://www.tiktok.com/t/ZTBo25VMF/),” especially “4. There is pleasure in riding the wave of resistance and alchemizing that energetic block into a sweet, delicioius flow.”
6. Most articles about reading more boil down to “put away your phone” and “read what you want, not what you think you should.” So I appreciated [this essay](https://johnpaulbrammer.substack.com/p/how-i-learned-to-read-way-way-more) about returning to reading not from a lack of attention but a lack of curiosity. (It also has that great tip about reading upstream — finding your influences’ influences).
7. I finished Stephen King’s [Bag of Bones](https://bookshop.org/p/books/bag-of-bones-a-novel-stephen-king/6a2d1ae46995c3c8?ean=9781501198892&next=t), my first [Big Book of Summer](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/big-books-for-summer) (which I’m considering is over 600 pages), and the end of the audiobook included a great interview with King that I can’t find online. I love a little nugget about high brow, popular, and lowbrow literature, but that all of it has to have a story — the engine that drives the book forward, because even a Ferrari without an engine — a book of great writing without a story —  can’t get you anywhere.
8. I used to read a lot of nature writing (Annie Dillard, Thoreau, “The Peregrine,” Edward Abbey) but all of them were a few generations before my time, so I really liked this [list of more recent nature writers](https://amybrunvand.substack.com/p/20-books-of-nature-writing-you-should), many of which I hadn’t heard of.
9. In praise of [goofy writing tips](https://www.newyorker.com/books/under-review/why-the-best-writing-advice-is-often-the-weirdest). If it works, it works!
10. This is way too niche to have mass appeal but the world needs more of that: I’ve been into Tolkien since I read the Hobbit in elementary school. I’ve also liked _some_ metal music, but this year have gotten into loud angry black metal (thanks ICE!). Anyways, [this metal concept album from Blind Guardian about The Silmarillion](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pG3u2TEdENQ&list=PLpSuFmBioWNHlL25rLGVFse_FOUu1BJZw&index=1) is so great.

See you on down the dusty trail,

Isak
