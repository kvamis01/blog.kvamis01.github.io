[Happy Friday](https://youtu.be/DfHvCrGAo1A),

I turned 33 years old which means I am officially of age in Hobbit years; I celebrated by staying up until 4 a.m. after saunaing with my dad — which was apparently too much fun because I’m still behind on sleep.

Here are 10 interesting things worth sharing this week:

1. I have a few print magazine subscriptions: “[Adventure Journal](https://www.adventure-journal.com/?srsltid=AfmBOorqmw9aMQLA5K0CxYQaIe8nsvBbO9aWfhF9ApZWnhXfoM9aUPKX),” an outdoorsy quarterly I signed up for when I quit my job to freelance write to get major inspiration from. And “[The New Yorker](https://www.newyorker.com/),” which I bought this year to spend less time reading the news on my phone. Analog is all the rage these days, but I my favorite part is receiving something nice in the mail instead of bills. Try it!
2. If you do subscribe, “The New Yorker” [fully digitized its entire back catalog](https://www.newyorker.com/archive). I went back and read issues from the week I was born, then my dad, then my grandpa. The fun thing about history is how all this has always been going on.
3. I may or may not have watched this [video about hobby tunneling](https://practical.engineering/blog/2026/2/17/so-you-want-to-build-a-tunnel?ref=thebrowser.com) and thought to myself: how hard could it _really_ be to dig my own tunnel from my basement to my detached garage?
4. A [new Bob Dylan book](https://www.amazon.com/After-Flood-Inside-Dylans-Memory/dp/0871402939) is came out in January about his later career, which might inspire me to get into more of it than just “Modern Times” and, yes, “Christmas in the Heart.”
5. The internet is better with RSS, and I missed the days of Google Reader so much I got a subscription to [Feed.ly](http://Feed.ly) (like don’t love) a few months ago. It seems there’s a bit of a resurgence lately — [Austin Kleon](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/the-best-way-to-read-the-internet) and [Cory Doctorow](https://pluralistic.net/2026/03/07/reader-mode/) both blogged recently about how much it improves your web browsing. I use it for work to track clean energy/climate news plus a bunch of writers and blogs I like.
6. I got my hands on the newest edition of [Webster’s Collegiate Dictionary](https://bookshop.org/p/books/merriam-webster-s-collegiate-dictionary-merriam-webster/d8573e99722ef1b5), the first update in 20 years. Cloth binding, nice pages, a thumb index — I’m in love, and it looks great next to my thesaurus and usage dictionary
7. I’m still in a book rut (this is a recurring theme apparently) and have been daydreaming reading Proust’s “In Search of Lost Time” after loving Knaussgaard’s “My Struggle” series a while back. So while I haven’t cracked the first book yet, I did discover the [Proust FM radio website](https://proustfm.com/) that plays the entire series on a loop forever.
8. If you feel like there’s maybe not much to celebrate for the U.S.’s 250th anniversary this year, well, you’re not alone — it seems like that’s how people have felt [for every major anniversary](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2026/03/09/scandal-protest-goofiness-and-grandeur-at-the-us-bicentennial) of the U.S. (And yes, I read this in print with a cup of coffee Tuesday morning instead of scrolling BlueSky.)
9. I’ve been poking around books instead of reading them all the way through this year. Last night I cracked open this beautiful edition of “[The Silmarillion](https://bookshop.org/p/books/the-silmarillion-illustrated-edition-illustrated-by-j-r-r-tolkien-j-r-r-tolkien/7e660d7339d9d0c2?ean=9780063280779&next=t)” and played [Andy Serkis’ narration](https://www.audible.com/pd/The-Silmarillion-Audiobook/B0C5MPMQYX) to read the Beren & Luthien chapter, and it was a WAY better night that scrolling Reddit, BlueSky, and Instagram.
10. I’ve been low-contact with podcasts since the pandemic because they just started to feel too noisy, but I might have to dive into the “[Exploring the Lord of the Rings](https://mythgard.org/lotro/exlotr/)” podcast series with the Tolkien Professor based on [this BlueSky thread](https://bsky.app/profile/dhmontgomery.com/post/3mgvtz3wxg22p) about the Mines of Moria.
