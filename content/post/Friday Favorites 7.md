[Happy Friday](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p3N2ZGSCOAw&list=RDp3N2ZGSCOAw&start_radio=1),

My creative antenna has been WAY more open than usual this week, and I’ve been riding it as long as it’ll push me, grateful each morning.

Here are 10 interesting things worth sharing this week:

1. I started my morning sitting in a chair, listening to the new [Bon Iver live album](https://open.spotify.com/album/2tV8MeptqoxJcuwQbh6Khr?si=0meT5thGTOe2QxMtR7SrqA), drinking a pot of coffee, and watching the sun melt the ice off the trees. Bliss. I love hearing artists rework their music to sound so different than the original studio version.
2. Summer is approaching, which means I need a [Big Summer Book](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/big-books-for-summer?utm_campaign=posts-open-in-app&triedRedirect=true&_src_ref=google.com). There’s something great about unfolding a huge book in June knowing warm months and many chapters lie ahead of you! [This list](https://caitlynrichardson.substack.com/p/big-books-that-are-actually-worth?utm_source=%2Finbox&utm_medium=reader2&utm_campaign=posts-open-in-app&triedRedirect=true) has helped me narrow down my top picks. Maybe it’s time to lug around a thick copy of “War and Peace” all summer [like Ladybird Johnson?](https://www.texasmonthly.com/arts-entertainment/lady-bird-2/) Or maybe a thick Stephen King book. (Maybe both?)
3. It’s [spring migration](https://www.audubon.org/washington/news/tuning-spring-migration) for my fellow birders! This year I got a phone with a better zoom lens and I LOVE IT for [photographing birds](https://bsky.app/profile/did:plc:s5wsdareb5srahm5bo3oy6vg/post/3micxm45tv22i). I don’t want to spend $5k on a good photography rig, so having a phone that doesn’t take potato-quality bird pics is great for sharing with friends.
4. Is creating art about finding meaning for ourselves or sharing an experience with others? [Lo-fi Cinema](https://youtu.be/lnFyx5lUKR4) on the joy of creating for both self-discovery and connection with others.
5. I’m listening to “[The Comfort Crisis](https://bookshop.org/p/books/the-comfort-crisis-embrace-discomfort-to-reclaim-your-wild-happy-healthy-self-michael-easter/ef6dfa9cc6a46d12)” while walking outside or doing chores and love the idea of a [Misogi](https://www.reddit.com/r/misogichallenge/comments/1htr8r0/what_is_a_misogi_challenge/): a hard task you undertake with roughly a 50/50 chance of success. The quirkier and more challenging the better, but do it for yourself, not social media. Reminds me many of Beau Miles’ adventures like when he commuted to work by [kayaking 4 days from his home to his office.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ysgH_rkfGSE) I’m a low-momentum homebody so this stuff fascinates me.
6. I was avoiding my important writing pieces this week by procrastinating on my phone or organizing my digital files. And while procrastinating, I noticed [Casey Neistat posted a new video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pdp3p23P-TI) about how procrastination and busy work are a necessary, integral part of the creative process, and I’ve felt less guilty about it.
7. I didn’t expect to tear up while scrolling my TikTok feed, nor did I expect such [a short video](https://www.tiktok.com/t/ZTk67ssYb/) to shift my perspective on childhood and adulthood.
8. “You give a chunk of the precious few hours of your life to something, imagining a great moment someday when it will all come together, and then when it does, you turn around and realize how many other great moments made up what you thought was ‘the process.’” [Brendan’s](https://semi-rad.com/2026/04/sure-i-trust-the-process-but/) always been great at celebrating how the little moments in our day end up being the most important thing we have. “How we spend our days is, of course, how we spend our lives,” says [Annie Dillard](https://www.themarginalian.org/2013/06/07/annie-dillard-the-writing-life-1/).
9. I’ve been reading Rick Rubin’s “[The Creative Act](https://bookshop.org/p/books/the-creative-act-a-way-of-being-rick-rubin/7884653d3a8189a4)” before bed in that consciousness window where you’re not fully awake but not quite asleep either. I have a surface-level understanding of Jung’s shadow self (check #4 above!), but I think there’s more communication going on with your subconscious in that half-dream state.
10. I’ve been making my way through Dante’s “Inferno,” and watching [lectures from Yale](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=679FGDpZBew), [Better Than Food](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mfOXhq9--kg), and [Brian McEvoy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9c0GJSnMOzY&t=30s&pp=ygUbZGFudGUncyBpbmZlcm5vIHZpZGVvIGVzc2F50gcJCdkKAYcqIYzv) has helped me better understand what I’m reading beyond “whoa, this is really graphic and messed up.” And don’t sleep on “keyword + lecture” videos with super-low views, those are sometimes the best ones.
