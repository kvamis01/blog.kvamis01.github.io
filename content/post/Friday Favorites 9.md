[Happy Friday](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bEgMvmWD1Y8&list=RDbEgMvmWD1Y8&start_radio=1&pp=ygUOZ29vZCwgcmVhbCBiZXKgBwE%3D),

I had a few days off work and enjoyed the unseasonably warm weather in Minnesota weeding the landscaping, detailing the car, and scrubbing my basement clean while listening to Peter Heller’s “[Celine](https://bookshop.org/p/books/celine-a-novel-peter-heller/54839c3e151be61f?ean=9781101973486&next=t)” and drinking a lot of Bustello coffee.

Here are 10 interesting things worth sharing this week:

1. “It was good to lie there in our bags watching the glow of our dying fire and the deeper glow of sunset beyond; but most of all it was good to feel the ground again and to know we were back in a country we loved,” said [Sigurd Olson](https://www.upress.umn.edu/9780816679089/wilderness-days/). It’s been a tough year for Minnesotans — and the latest attack on our [Boundary Waters](https://www.friends-bwca.org/blog/senate-passes-hjr-140-boundary-waters-what-comes-next/) is enraging.
2. Big fan of making lists, and love these [archive photos of Richard Feynman’s notes](https://substack.com/home/post/p-188278785), including a “Notebook of Things I Don’t Know About.”
3. “The feeling that the work is magnificent, and the feeling that it is abominable, are both mosquitoes to be repelled, ignored, or killed, but not indulged.” [Annie Dillard](https://www.themarginalian.org/2013/08/09/annie-dillard-on-writing/) always has great, no-nonsense writing advice. I think if I spent 1/10 the time just creating stuff instead of feeling and thinking about creating stuff, I’d have a much better time.
4. On that note: “Worrying isn’t writing, and you can only revise drafts, not worries.” Advice I need to remind myself over and over again. [Writing with Andrew](https://youtu.be/XFvJRuvjMMM)’s speaking and presentation style is so similar to [Technology Connections](https://youtu.be/YE0U018Copw) I googled if they were brothers or something.
5. Speaking of creative procrastination and typewriters: I’ve been ogling the different distraction-free writing set-ups at [r/writerdecks](https://www.reddit.com/r/writerDeck/). (For now, it’s my [keys-to-go keyboard](https://www.amazon.com/Logitech-Super-Slim-Super-Light-Bluetooth-Including/dp/B00R0I71S4) and [Notion](https://www.notion.com/) against the world.)
6. David Byrne has a magical, weird way of making everyday routines and items feel unique and creative and interesting to me, so of course [this long interview with Nardwuar](https://youtu.be/qLBXPlgKxNg) — who has a unique interview style of his own — has been a delight to watch.
7. I’ve been missing the bologna sandwiches, PBR, and Moonpies of [Robert’s](https://www.instagram.com/robertswesternworld?igsh=MXBubzFnMjFxZ2puNw==) in Nashville, so I’ve been listening to old country music like [Ernest Tubbs](https://music.apple.com/us/album/thanks-a-lot/1482693760?i=1482693920) all week.
8. I generally don’t mind spoilers for books and movies, but I was flabbergasted at reading a [book about the Transcendentalists](https://bookshop.org/p/books/the-emerson-circle-the-concord-radicals-who-reinvented-the-world-bruce-nichols/f0e37069a0311750?ean=9781668094877&next=t), finding Margaret Fuller to be the coolest of the bunch, and then being gutted to learn she and her book manuscript were lost in a shipwreck so early in her career.
9. I liked Richard Powers’ “[The Overstory](https://bookshop.org/p/books/the-overstory-richard-powers/e155c5f9d6ae4c60?ean=9780393356687&next=t)” (and loved Anthony Doerr’s “[Cloud Cuckoo Land](https://bookshop.org/p/books/cloud-cuckoo-land-a-novel-anthony-doerr/3b4cd9735fd83bef?ean=9781982168445&next=t)”), and both feel very similar to [this essay from Robert Moor](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2026/03/09/the-tree-house-and-the-oil-pipeline)’s upcoming book about climate activists that lived in a treehouse for months to block an oil pipeline. Been thinking about it for a month.
10. Twice a year LitHub publishes what I call “[the big list of cool, new books](https://lithub.com/lit-hubs-most-anticipated-books-of-2026/),” and I discover lots of interesting books I wouldn’t otherwise find.
